# Governance controls: audit trail & retention

If you own data-quality compliance, these are the controls you'll be asked about in an audit: who changed what, who approved it, and how long the evidence and any sensitive rows are kept. This page explains both — the audit trail and the retention settings — so you can answer those questions with confidence.

Both are configured in Admin Settings and can only be changed by an admin. See [Set up DQX Studio for your organization](/docs/studio/start-here/set-up-for-your-org).

## Audit trail & change history

Every change to a rule, a role assignment, or a schedule is recorded — who made it, when, and the before-and-after values. Nothing that governs your data quality changes silently.

This is your evidence trail. It answers the questions an auditor will ask:

- **"Who granted this group the ability to run checks?"** Role and permission changes are logged, including which workspace group was mapped to which app role and when.
- **"Who approved this rule, and when?"** Every rule moves through draft → submit → review → approve before it goes active. That approval is recorded against the person who made it.
- **"What did this rule look like before it was edited?"** Changes keep a before-and-after record, so you can see exactly what a rule, threshold, or schedule was previously set to.

Approval history is visible on each item itself. When you open a rule, a monitored table, or a collection, you can see who approved it and read any comments the approver left when they signed off — so the reasoning behind a decision travels with the item, not just the fact that it happened.

<Admonition type="info" title="The approvals policy is itself audited">
Whether approval is required at all is a governed control. Turning the four-eyes approval requirement on or off is recorded like any other change, so no one can quietly relax the review policy. See [The approval workflow](/docs/studio/governance/approval-workflow).
</Admonition>

## Data retention & sensitive rows

You control how long DQX Studio keeps its data with two separate retention settings, because two very different kinds of data are involved.

- **Runs, results, and metrics** — how long the history of your checks is kept: the scores, the trends over time, and the pass/fail counts. This is your long-term quality record, so it typically has a longer retention.
- **Quarantined sample rows** — how long the actual failing rows are kept.

**Quarantine** is a small sample of the rows that failed a check, captured so someone can inspect *why* they failed. Because those rows come from your real data, they may contain sensitive or personal (PII) information. That's why quarantine has its own, usually shorter, retention: you keep the evidence just long enough to investigate a problem, then it's cleared — supporting data minimization.

### Who can see quarantined rows

Access to quarantined sample rows is limited to approvers and admins. On top of that, you only ever see data you already have permission to in Unity Catalog — DQX Studio can't show you rows from a table you couldn't otherwise read. For the full picture of how the app respects your existing access, see [Data access & security](/docs/studio/governance/data-access-and-security).

## Governance FAQ

**Can an author approve their own rule?**
No — not unless they are also an approver or admin. This is the four-eyes rule: the person who writes a change and the person who approves it should be different people. See [The approval workflow](/docs/studio/governance/approval-workflow).

**Who can see quarantined data?**
Approvers and admins, and only for data they already have access to in Unity Catalog. See [Data access & security](/docs/studio/governance/data-access-and-security).

**What is logged, and for how long?**
Every change to rules, roles, and schedules is logged with who, when, and before/after values. Runs, results, and metrics are kept for the period an admin sets; quarantined sample rows are kept for a separate, usually shorter, period. Both are set in Admin Settings.

**What identity runs a scheduled check?**
Checks run against only the data the app has access to, and you only see catalogs and tables you can already read in Unity Catalog. See [Data access & security](/docs/studio/governance/data-access-and-security).

**Who can change these settings?**
Only admins, in Admin Settings. See [Set up DQX Studio for your organization](/docs/studio/start-here/set-up-for-your-org).
