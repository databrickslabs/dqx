# Try the built-in demo

DQX Studio ships with a **built-in demo** so you can explore every feature
against realistic data before pointing it at your own tables. It creates a small
set of sample tables, a library of reusable rules, monitored tables, collections,
and past runs — enough to see scores, trends, failing records, and the approval
workflow in action.

## Deploy the demo

An admin turns the demo on from **Admin Settings**. Deploying it sets up the
sample catalog, seeds the rules and monitored tables, and generates a history of
runs so the results views have something to show.

<Admonition type="note" title="Admin only">
Only the **Admin** role can deploy or remove the demo, since it creates sample
data and app content. See
[Set up DQX Studio for your organization](/docs/studio/governance/).
</Admonition>

## What you can explore

Once the demo is in place, walk through the whole product with real content:

- Browse the [rules registry](/docs/studio/authoring/create-a-rule) and see how
  rules are reused across tables.
- Open a [monitored table](/docs/studio/monitoring/monitor-a-table) and its
  [results page](/docs/studio/running/results) — including failing records you
  can inspect.
- View a [collection](/docs/studio/monitoring/collections) such as *Customer 360*
  to see quality across related tables at once.
- Try [Ask Genie](/docs/studio/running/genie) on the demo's results.

It's the fastest way for a new steward or reviewer to get comfortable — nothing
they do to the demo touches production data.

## Remove it when you're done

The demo can be removed from Admin Settings just as easily. Removing it clears
the sample content so it doesn't clutter your workspace once your team is
working with real tables.
