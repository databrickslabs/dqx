# Measure & report quality health

This page is for anyone who needs to answer "how healthy is our data?" — and
especially for Data Governance Managers who report that answer up to leadership.
It shows you where to find the numbers, how to read them, and what to quote in a
review.

## What "healthy" means

Data health in DQX Studio comes down to one number: the **DQ score**. It's the
share of tests that passed.

A **test** is a single evaluation of one check against one row (or one aggregate)
during a run. A **check** is a rule applied to a specific table or column, and a
**rule** is the reusable definition behind it. So when a run reports
"421,121 failed of 2,350,000 tests", that's counting row-level evaluations — not
rules. One rule checking a million rows produces a million tests.

<Admonition type="info" title="Rules, checks, and tests">
If those three words blur together, the
[Key concepts](/docs/studio/start-here/key-concepts#rules-checks-and-tests) page
lays them out with examples. It's worth two minutes before your first report.
</Admonition>

A score is **green** when it's at or above the threshold you've set for it, and
flagged **below threshold** when it falls short. Green means "meeting the bar we
agreed on" — not necessarily 100%. You choose the bar (see
[Set quality thresholds](/docs/studio/monitoring/quality-thresholds)).

## Where to see health

You can look at quality health at four levels, from a single table up to your
whole workspace. Pick the one that matches the question you're answering.

### 1. The home page, at a glance

The Studio home page gives you the headline: your overall DQ score and how it's
trending. Start here for a quick pulse check.

### 2. A single monitored table's Results

Open any monitored table and go to its **Results** tab to see the score for that
one table — its trend, its breakdowns, and the rows that failed. Use this when
someone asks about a specific dataset.

### 3. A collection's combined Results

A **collection** is a data product: a group of related tables you view and run
together (for example, the tables behind a Genie space or a published data
product). Its **Results** tab is the view to use when you report on the health of
a whole data product.

It combines the score across every member table, shows the trend over time, and
breaks the score down by dimension, severity, rule, and table. From any breakdown
you can drill down to the actual failing rows.

<Admonition type="tip" title="This is the money view for reporting">
When leadership asks "how's the customer data?", the collection Results score is
the number to quote. It rolls the whole data product into one figure with a trend
line to back it up. See [Collections](/docs/studio/monitoring/collections) to
group your tables.
</Admonition>

### 4. Global Results, workspace-wide (optional)

An admin can turn on a **Global Results** tab that shows quality trends across the
entire workspace — every monitored table in one place. Use it for org-wide
reporting. If you don't see it, it hasn't been enabled; ask your admin.

## Reading the breakdowns

A single score tells you *whether* things are healthy. The breakdowns tell you
*where* the problem is.

- **By dimension** — which quality dimension is weakest: completeness, validity,
  accuracy, consistency, uniqueness, or timeliness. If validity is dragging you
  down, you know values are out of range or malformed rather than missing.
- **By severity** — how much of the failure is **errors** (which fail the run)
  versus **warnings** (reported but not blocking). Errors first.
- **By rule** — your worst offenders. The rules failing the most tests are where
  fixing one thing moves the score the most.
- **By table** — which dataset in a collection is holding the score back.

## Ask Genie

Every Results view has an **Ask Genie** box: type a plain-English question about
your quality data and get an answer back. For example, "which table dropped the
most this week?" or "what's failing most on the orders table?" It's a quick way to
interrogate the numbers without building anything.

## Trends over time

The trend line is what turns a number into a story for stakeholders.

- **Spot regressions early.** A score that steps down after a run is your signal
  to look — usually a new data source or an upstream change. Follow the drop into
  the by-rule or by-table breakdown to find the cause.
- **Show improvement.** When a team fixes a data issue, the trend line climbs and
  stays up. That's the evidence that quality work is paying off — far more
  convincing in a review than a single snapshot.

<Admonition type="tip" title="What to put in a leadership report">
Quote the current score and the direction of the trend, not just today's figure.
"Customer 360 is at 82.7%, up from 78% last month" says more than either number
alone. Pair it with the top one or two failing rules so the conversation moves
straight to action.
</Admonition>

## When a score is red

Seeing a below-threshold score is the start, not the end. To act on a failing
check — inspect the failing rows and get the rule right — see
[Fix a failing rule](/docs/studio/running/fix-a-failing-rule).

<Admonition type="note" title="Your data is never changed">
Reviewing and drilling into failures never modifies your data. Failing rows are
sampled into a read-only view for triage; DQX Studio only reads and evaluates.
</Admonition>
